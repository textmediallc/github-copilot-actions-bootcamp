## Lab 5: Installing and Configuring GitHub Copilot
**Real-World Scenario:** The company has just purchased Copilot licenses to increase developer velocity. You need to configure it correctly in your IDE.
**Why we are doing this:** Proper configuration ensures Copilot works seamlessly without being intrusive, and understanding the settings helps manage privacy and context.

### Step-by-Step Instructions:
1. **Install the Extensions:**
   * Open the VS Code Extensions view (`Ctrl+Shift+X`).
   * Search for "GitHub Copilot Chat" and make sure it is installed.
2. **Authenticate Copilot:**
   * A prompt should appear in the bottom right asking you to sign in to GitHub. Click it and follow the browser prompts.
3. **Configure Settings:**
   * Open VS Code Settings (`Ctrl+,`).
   * Search for `github.copilot.enable`.
   * Ensure it is enabled for your preferred languages (e.g., `*` for all, or specifically `markdown`, `javascript`, etc.).
4. **Verify the Status:**
   * Look for the Copilot icon (the small face) in the bottom right status bar. If it's visible and not crossed out, Copilot is active.
